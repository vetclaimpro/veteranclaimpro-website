The Reality

The VA Claims Process Is Designed to Be Hard

Most veterans are owed far more than they receive. Not because their service wasn't real — but because navigating the VA's claims system without the right documentation, the right language, and the right preparation is like going into battle without a weapon.

01

File an Initial Claim

You submit your claim online or by paper. You need to list every service-connected condition, gather medical records, and provide a personal statement. Most veterans don't know what to include — so they leave money on the table before the process even begins.

02

C&P Exam (Compensation & Pension)

The VA schedules you for a C&P Exam — a medical evaluation that can make or break your rating. Examiners are often rushed, and if you don't know exactly what to say and how to say it, your exam results can devastate your claim. This exam is not a treatment — it's an evidence-gathering session, and you need to be prepared like it's a deposition.

03

The VA Makes a Decision

Months later — sometimes over a year — you receive a rating decision. Many veterans are rated far too low, or have conditions denied entirely, because the medical evidence wasn't presented in the specific format the VA requires. A nexus letter from your doctor stating service connection in VA-accepted language could mean the difference between 0% and 70%.

04

Denial or Low Rating

If you're denied or under-rated, you have three appeal lanes: Supplemental Claim, Higher-Level Review, or Board of Veterans' Appeals. Each requires different documentation, different strategies, and precise legal language. Without knowing these paths, veterans wait years and give up.

05

Secondary Conditions

Most veterans don't realize that conditions caused or worsened by a service-connected disability — like sleep apnea caused by PTSD, or knee issues caused by a service-connected back condition — can each receive their own rating. Missing these means leaving thousands of dollars per month on the table, every single month, for the rest of your life.

⚠ What Can Go Wrong at Every Step

✕

Incomplete initial claim — Missing conditions or weak personal statements lead to immediate denials or lowball ratings.

✕

No nexus letter — Without a letter connecting your condition to your service in VA-approved language, your claim has no medical foundation.

✕

Unprepared for C&P exam — Underselling your symptoms in a 15-minute exam costs you rating points you can never get back without a lengthy appeal.

✕

Missing C-File evidence — Your C-File contains your entire VA history. Errors, missing records, and overlooked documentation inside it are silently tanking your rating.

✕

Not tracking your Intent to File — Miss your ITF window and you lose back pay going back to your filing date. That can mean losing tens of thousands of dollars.

✕

No buddy letter, no witness — A buddy letter from someone who witnessed your condition adds crucial lay evidence. Most veterans never get one because the process is complicated and awkward to coordinate.

✕

Giving up after denial — Over 60% of initial claims are denied or under-rated. The veterans who win are the ones who know how to appeal — and what documents to file when they do.

⚠ "I Had That Before I Joined" — You May Still Have a Claim

One of the most common reasons veterans never file is the belief that if a condition existed before they entered service, they can't claim it. That is not how VA law works.

Under 38 CFR, the VA recognizes a category called "aggravation of a pre-existing condition." If you entered service with a condition — a bad knee, a back problem, a mental health issue — and your time in service made it measurably worse beyond its natural progression, you are entitled to compensation for that aggravation. You don't have to prove the military caused it from scratch. You only have to show that service made it worse.

Not all VA claims are for injuries or illnesses that originated during service. Chronic conditions worsened by physical demands, occupational exposure, deployment stress, or military lifestyle all qualify. If you've been sitting on a condition because you assumed it "didn't count," it's time to take a second look — and VeteranClaimPro's Secondary Conditions Finder and Claims Coach can help you build the case.

Common Questions

Is this a one-time purchase or a subscription?+
The app is free to download, and Demo Mode lets you explore every feature before paying anything. To unlock Pro — full, unlimited access to every generator, coach, and tool — it's a one-time $99.99 in-app purchase. Paid once, yours forever. No monthly fees, no annual renewals, no per-document charges. When we release updates, you get them as part of your purchase.
Does this replace a VSO or attorney?+
VeteranClaimPro is a powerful tool that helps you understand your claim, generate documentation, and prepare for every step of the process. It does not provide legal or medical advice, and does not replace a licensed attorney or accredited VSO for complex appeals. What it does is make sure you walk into every interaction fully prepared, with the right documents in hand.
How accurate are the documents the app generates?+
Every document is built by collecting your specific information through structured, guided questions — then assembling it using language frameworks drawn from VA-approved forms, CFR regulations, and established claim standards. The Nexus Letter, Personal Statement, and Appeal Letter generators are built to produce VA-formatted output using the exact terminology raters and examiners look for. Documents should always be reviewed before submission to ensure they accurately reflect your personal situation.
What is a C-File and how does the analyzer work?+
Your C-File (Claims File) is the complete VA record of your service history, medical records, and previous claims decisions. It can contain errors, missing records, and undiscovered evidence that is silently affecting your rating. The app walks you through how to request your C-File from the VA, and includes a guided analysis tool that systematically cross-references your file against known claim criteria to help you identify gaps and discrepancies. The How To Use guide in the app includes a 4-step process for getting and using your C-File.
Is Android available?+
iOS is available now. The Android version is fully built with feature parity to iOS, and we're completing the Google Play approval process. Message us with "Android" in the subject and we'll notify you the moment it launches.
What is the Buddy Letter Share feature?+
A Buddy Letter (also called a lay statement) is a written account from someone who witnessed your condition or its effects — a fellow service member, family member, or friend. It can be powerful evidence for your claim. The app generates a shareable link you send to your buddy. They fill out the form on their phone — no app download required. Once submitted, the completed letter saves automatically to your Documents inside the app.
What is an Intent to File (ITF) and why does it matter?+
Filing an Intent to File with the VA establishes your effective date — the date from which back pay is calculated if your claim is approved. If your claim takes 12 months to process and you never filed an ITF, you may lose a year of back pay. The app includes an ITF tracker with a countdown timer and a direct link to the VA.gov ITF form so you never miss this critical step.
Can I try the app before purchasing?+
Yes. The app includes a Demo Mode that lets you explore every feature and generator before committing to a purchase. You'll see exactly what the app does — and what it can do for your claim — before spending a dollar.
